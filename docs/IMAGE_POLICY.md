# Nagotosha Image Policy

Updated: 2026-07-09

## Purpose

Nagotosha is a visual, mobile-first local media product. Articles should be easy to understand quickly through images, not only through text.

This policy defines how images are selected, credited, and used in Nagotosha articles.

## Basic Policy

- Articles should be planned with images from the beginning.
- Do not choose an article only because it can work without images.
- Eyecatch images should generally come from official, PR, or otherwise usable image sources.
- Every image needs a clear source or credit.
- Generated images should not be the default main image.
- Generated images may be used only for roundups, fallback visuals, or editorial illustration when a self-made image is clearly appropriate.
- If image permission or usage conditions are unclear, pause the article.

## Image Source Priority

Use image sources in this order when possible:

1. Official PR materials provided by the store, facility, organizer, or brand
2. Distributed PR images, such as PR TIMES images, with source attribution
3. Tourism association or local government photo libraries
4. Images directly provided by stores or facilities
5. Nagotosha's own photography
6. Images created by the Nagotosha editorial team
7. Never use images with unknown sources

## Prohibited Uses

Do not use:

- Unapproved copies of images from official websites
- Images copied from news sites
- Images copied from SNS without permission
- Hotlinked external images
- Images with unclear usage conditions uploaded into WordPress media
- Photos where people are prominently visible without rights confirmation
- Logos or character images that have been modified without permission
- Cropped, edited, or recolored images when the source terms prohibit alteration

## Credit Notation

Use clear credit text near the image or in `ArticleExperience.imageCredit`.

Examples:

- `鐢诲儚鎻愪緵: 鈼嬧棆`
- `鐢诲儚鍑哄吀: 鈼嬧棆鍏紡銈点偆銉坄
- `鐢诲儚鎻愪緵: 锛堝叕璨★級鍚嶅彜灞嬭Τ鍏夈偝銉炽儥銉炽偡銉с兂銉撱儱銉笺儹銉糮
- `鐢诲儚鍑哄吀: 鏍紡浼氱ぞ鈼嬧棆 / PR TIMES`
- `鐢诲儚鍑哄吀: 銇仈銇ㄣ仐銈冪法闆嗛儴浣滄垚銇偆銉°兗銈竊

Credit should appear:

- below the image in article body, or
- in the ArticleExperience hero/image credit area, or
- in a nearby card when the image is used inside a custom article layout

## Pre-Article Checklist

Before starting a new article, confirm:

- Is there a usable image?
- Have the image usage conditions been checked?
- Has the credit notation been decided?
- Is cropping or editing allowed?
- Is the image allowed for web publication?
- Is the image allowed for SNS sharing if the article will be promoted?
- Are there people, copyrighted characters, logos, or artworks that create extra rights risk?
- If no safe image is available, should the article be postponed?

## Photo Library Rules

When using a tourism or public photo library:

- Confirm whether registration is required.
- Follow the exact credit notation rules.
- Check whether a post-publication URL report is required.
- Do not remove watermarks.
- Do not crop or edit images when prohibited.
- Save the source URL and usage terms in the draft notes when possible.

Example:

- Nagoya Concierge Photo Library may require registration, credit notation, and post-publication URL reporting. Follow its current usage rules before using any image.

## Operational Rules

- Check images before writing the article body.
- Do not move to draft writing until the image plan is safe.
- If image usage is unclear, mark the candidate as pending.
- Do not mass-produce image-free articles.
- Safe archive cleanup is an exception: existing low-priority articles may be made safer by removing risky images and keeping them as text-focused archive posts.
- Existing article safety work does not mean new articles can be image-free.

## Layout Implications

Every article candidate should map to a visual layout:

- `store`: single shop or facility with safe shop/food/place images
- `news`: opening, renewal, event, or announcement with official/PR visuals
- `guide`: evergreen guide with safe editorial, provided, or self-created images
- `feature`: seasonal or roundup story with strong visual lead image
- `safe archive`: existing article cleanup only; not a preferred format for new production

## When to Stop

Stop article production when:

- the only available images are from unrelated news sites
- the only available images are SNS reposts
- official images exist but usage permission is not clear
- the image contains people and permission is unclear
- the image depends on a character, logo, or artwork and alteration or reuse is unclear
- the image would require hotlinking

In these cases, request permission, find a safer source, or postpone the article.
